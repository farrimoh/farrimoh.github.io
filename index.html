Assembly of Cargo Encapsulating Nanoshells

Cargo-encapsulating nanoshells serve as potential drug delivery carriers. I used coarse-grained and theoretical modeling to uncover how cargo, scaffolds, and shell mechanics together control their size, morphology, and self-assembly pathways.

  • Mechanisms of scaffold-mediated microcompartment assembly and size control. F. Mohajerani, E. Sayer, C. Neil, K. Inlow, M.F. Hagan. ACS Nano 15 (3), 4197-4212 (2021).
  • The role of the encapsulated cargo in microcompartment assembly. F. Mohajerani, M.F. Hagan. PLoS Computational Biology 14 (7), e1006351 (2018).
  • Many-molecule encapsulation by an icosahedral shell. J.D. Perlmutter, F. Mohajerani, M.F. Hagan. eLife 5, e14078 (2016).
  • Microcompartment assembly around multicomponent fluid cargoes. L. Tsidilkovski, F. Mohajerani, M.F. Hagan. Journal of Chemical Physics 156 (24) (2022).

Liquid-liquid Phase Separation (LLPS) in Biological Systems

We developed a kinetic and thermodynamic framework showing how liquid-liquid phase separation can dramatically enhance reversible self-assembly by concentrating subunits within condensates and expanding the range of conditions for efficient assembly.

  • Self-assembly coupled to liquid-liquid phase separation. M.F. Hagan, F. Mohajerani. PLoS Computational Biology 19 (5), e1010652 (2023).
